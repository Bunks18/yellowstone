Father and Son Trip

Grand Tetons and Yellowstone

As a college graduation present I organized a ten-day trip to two of my favorite national parks for my son Will, his two best friends, and their fathers. We arrived in Jackson and stayed at Signal Mountain Lodge, right on Jackson Lake with stunning views of the ‘Grand’. We hiked to Inspiration Point and Phelps Lake, then explored downtown Jackson where the boys purchased their cowboys hats.

We drove to Yellowstone and spotted our first grizzly in the Hayden Valley . We continued on our way to visit the Mud Pots, Grand Canyon of Yellowstone , Tower Falls, Roosevelt Lodge, and Mammoth Hot Springs. We spent the next four evenings at the Yellowstone Association (YA) Overlook Cabins in Gardiner, MT. Our YA interpretative guide organized day hikes in the Northern Range/Lamar Valley where we viewed wolves, elk, bison, pronghorn, eagles, and moose, and learned about the natural history of this region of the Park. I managed to sneak off for several hours to fly fish on the Firehole and Gibbon rivers.

We drove to our next destination at the Old Faithful Village and on our way stopped to explore Norris Basin, Lower Geyser Basin, Mystic Falls, and the Midway Geyser Basin. Spending two-days in the Old Faithful area, we hiked to Fairy Falls and Lone Star Geyser and along the Howard Eaton Trail. Our final evening together in Jackson included a round of drinks at the Cowboy Bar and an hour of our ‘lessons learned and highlights’ from the journals we all kept of our travels. Priceless.
